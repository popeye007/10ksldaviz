The Company manages its properties in an attempt to capture its highest and best use and customarily does not sell property until it determines that the property is surplus to its agricultural activities by reason of its potential for industrial, commercial or residential use. The Company has little control over when this occurs as real estate sales are primarily market driven. The Company carries large receivables from seller-financed sales of large tracts of surplus land the collectibility of which is subject to credit risk relating to debtors. The Company is subject to environmental liability by virtue of owning significant holdings of real estate assets. The Company has three large customers that account for 46% of revenues. Agricultural products are subject to supply and demand pricing which is not predictable. The Company s agricultural assets are concentrated and the effects of adverse weather conditions such as hurricanes can be exaggerated. Pesticide and herbicide use by the Company or its lessees could create liability for the Company.
