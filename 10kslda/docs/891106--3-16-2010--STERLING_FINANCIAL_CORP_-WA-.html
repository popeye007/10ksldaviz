We are undercapitalized and face a number of challenges in the near term future. We may fail to raise additional capital, which may result in our filing for bankruptcy and/or our banking subsidiaries entering FDIC receivership, and our Independent Registered Public Accounting Firm has expressed substantial doubt about our ability to continue as a going concern. The uncertainty of our financial viability could result in a loss of access to funding sources, which would have a negative impact on our liquidity and may cause our regulators to place us in receivership before we have implemented our strategic alternatives. We incurred significant losses over the last five quarters, and may continue do so in the future, and we can make no assurances as to when we will be profitable. Sterling s stock price has declined and Treasury s letter requires that capital be raised at no more than $0.20 cents per share, which is likely to impact the price. Any recapitalization would have a significant dilutive effect. Our banking subsidiary, Sterling Savings Bank, has entered into a consent agreement with the FDIC and the WDFI, and Sterling has entered into a written agreement with the Federal Reserve. Sterling has not complied with the deadline established by the FDIC and the WDFI to raise additional capital. The SSB Consent Agreement limits the types of funding sources on which we may rely and may have a negative impact on our liquidity. We may be deemed to be in default with regard to certain of our borrowing agreements as a result of the receipt of the SSB Consent Agreement and the Reserve Bank Agreement. Our estimated allowance for losses in our loan portfolio may be inadequate, which would cause our results of operations and financial condition to be adversely affected. We have received a notice of noncompliance from the NASDAQ Stock Market and our common stock may be subject to delisting. The effects of the current economic recession have been particularly severe in our primary market areas in the Pacific Northwest and Northern California. A large percentage of our loan portfolio is secured by real estate. Continued deterioration in the real estate market or other segments of our loan portfolio would lead to additional losses, which could have a material adverse effect on our business, financial condition and results of operations. We have a concentration in higher risk non owner occupied commercial real estate loans. Commercial loans may involve a higher degree of risk. A rapid change in interest rates could make it difficult to maintain our current net interest income spread and could result in reduced earnings. Our cost of funds may increase as a result of many factors, which may reduce our profitability. We may have reduced access to wholesale funding sources. Our banking operations are subject to extensive governmental regulation and further regulatory actions against us may impair our operations or restrict our growth. Difficult market conditions have adversely affected and may continue to have an adverse effect on our industry. Sterling has deferred regularly scheduled interest payments on its outstanding junior subordinated notes relating to its trust preferred securities (collectively, the TruPS ) and may be deemed to be in default on the TruPS if payment is not made prior to the end of the deferral period. Sterling may be required to bring its deferred TruPS payments current in order repurchase some or all of its outstanding TruPS obligations and/or may be unable to complete its tender offer for the TruPS. Sterling may realize losses on its investment securities in future periods if management decides not to hold the investments to recovery, there is a change in management s or the rating agencies assessment of credit risk on individual securities, or a change in regulatory or accounting requirements. As a bank holding company that conducts substantially all of our operations through our banking subsidiaries, Sterling Savings Bank and Golf Savings Bank, our ability to pay dividends, repurchase our shares or to repay our indebtedness depends upon liquid assets held by the holding company and the results of operations of our subsidiaries. Emergency measures designed to stabilize the U.S. financial markets are beginning to wind down. We are currently precluded from making any dividend payments on our common stock. Because of our participation in TARP, we are subject to restrictions on compensation paid to our executives. Sterling may not be able to replace key members of management or attract and retain qualified relationship managers in the future. Because of our participation in TARP, we are subject to restrictions on our ability to repurchase our shares. The financial services industry is subject to extensive regulation. The financial services industry is highly competitive. The value of the securities in our investment securities portfolio may be negatively affected by continued disruptions in securities markets. A substantial decline in the value of our FHLB common stock may result in an other-than-temporary impairment charge. Current and future increases in FDIC insurance premiums, including the FDIC special assessment imposed on all FDIC-insured institutions, will decrease our earnings. Our business is highly reliant on technology and our ability to manage the operational risks associated with technology. Changes in accounting standards may have a material impact on how we report our financial condition and results of operations. We may be required to repurchase mortgage loans in some circumstances, which could harm our liquidity, results of operations and financial condition. Sterling operations could be interrupted if its third-party service providers experience difficulty, terminate their services or fail to comply with banking regulations. Sterling s internal control systems could fail to detect certain events. The network and computer systems on which Sterling depends could fail or experience a security breach. Sterling could be held responsible for environmental liabilities of properties acquired through foreclosure. We are currently subject to certain pending shareholder litigation and may be subject to similar claims in the future.
