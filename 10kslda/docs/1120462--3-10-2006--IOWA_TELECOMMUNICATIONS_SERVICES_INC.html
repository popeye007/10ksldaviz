We have substantial indebtedness and may incur additional indebtedness in the future, which could restrict our ability to pay dividends. We may not be able to refinance our credit facilities at maturity on favorable terms or at all. We will require a significant amount of cash, which may not be available to us, to service our debt, pay dividends and fund our other liquidity needs. There may be volatility in the trading price of our common stock, which could negatively affect the value of your investment. Future sales, or the possibility of future sales, of a substantial amount of our common stock may depress the price of the shares of our common stock. Your interests may conflict with those of our current shareholders with whom we conduct significant business. Our organizational documents could limit another party s ability to acquire us and therefore could deprive our investors of the opportunity to obtain a takeover premium for their shares. Risks Relating to Our Business and Industry Competition in the telecommunications industry could result in access line losses or reduce our customer base, possibly requiring that we lower our rates, increase marketing expenditures, invest in new technologies or capabilities or use discounting and promotional campaigns that adversely affect our margins. We may in the future compete with the Iowa Communications Network or with a future purchaser of the assets now owned by the Iowa Communications Network. We may continue to lose access lines due to economic conditions and competition. We may not be able to integrate future technologies, respond effectively to customer requirements or provide new services. Network disruptions could adversely affect our operating results. Our relationships with other telecommunications companies are material to our operations and their financial difficulties may affect our business. We face risks associated with our reliance on our information and billing systems. We depend on key members of our senior management team. New regulations and changes in existing regulations may force us to incur significant expenses. As the incumbent local exchange carrier in our service areas, we are subject to regulation that is not applicable to our competitors. Service outage reporting may result in substantial expenditures.
