We depend heavily on U.S. government contracts, which are subject to unique risks. We enter into fixed-price contracts, which could subject us to losses if we have cost overruns. We use estimates in accounting for many contracts and programs. Changes in our estimates could adversely affect our future financial results. Significant changes in discount rates, actual investment return on pension assets and other factors could affect our earnings, equity, and pension contributions in future periods.
